Project 4

Daily Cash Flow Dashboard: Multi-Store Retail Analysis (Power BI & Google Sheets)

Overview:
Developed a robust daily cash flow management system for two retail stores by integrating Google Sheets (for data entry) with Power BI (for visualization and analysis). This solution automates daily tracking of cash inflows, outflows, deposits, and discrepancies, providing real-time insights for better decision-making.

  • Automated data import from Google Sheets to Power BI for real-time dashboard updates.
  • Daily tracking of sales, expenses, coin additions, and deposits for each store.
  • Dynamic calculation of opening/closing balances, shortfall/excess, and cash left in drawer.
  • Custom Power BI dashboards for trend analysis, store comparisons, and anomaly detection.

How Daily Cash Flow Works:

1. Opening Balance:
At the beginning of each day, the cashier starts with an Opening Balance from the previous day's closing balance. This amount is carried over from the previous day’s end and represents the cash available to the cashier to begin the day’s operations.
Example: If yesterday’s closing balance was ₹5,000, this becomes today’s opening balance.

2. Cash Sales:
Throughout the day, as customers make cash purchases, the cashier records the amount. This cash is added to the Opening Balance to calculate the total cash available for the day.
Example: If today's cash sales total ₹8,000, then the total cash available becomes ₹5,000 (opening balance) + ₹8,000 (cash sales) = ₹13,000.

3. Expenses (Store and Staff Payments):
The store incurs daily expenses such as rent, electricity, and staff payments. These expenses are deducted from the total available cash.
Example: If store expenses amount to ₹2,000 and staff payments total ₹3,000, this ₹5,000 will be deducted from the total cash available.
The amount left after expenses is referred to as the Cash After Expenses.

4. Closing Balance:
After all expenses are deducted, the remaining cash is the Closing Balance. This is the final amount the cashier has at the end of the day, ready for deposit or carryover.
Example: If, after deducting ₹5,000 in expenses, ₹8,000 is remaining, this ₹8,000 becomes the closing balance.

5. Deposit:
At the end of the day, a portion of the closing balance is deposited into the store’s bank account. This is done to ensure that the store does not hold too much cash overnight.
Example: If ₹6,000 is deposited into the bank, the remaining ₹2,000 stays with the cashier for the next day.

6. Next Day’s Opening Balance:
The Opening Balance for the next day is the remaining amount after the deposit is made.
Example: If the closing balance was ₹8,000 and ₹6,000 was deposited, the next day’s opening balance will be ₹2,000.

Key Google Sheets Formula Example:

Google Sheets Formula:

=LEFT_TO_DRAWER + DEPOSIT - CLOSING_BALANCE
= D7346 + D7345 - D7342

Power BI DAX Measure:

Closing Balance = 
SUM('CashFlow'[Opening Balance]) +
SUM('CashFlow'[Total Income]) -
SUM('CashFlow'[Total Expenses]) -
SUM('CashFlow'[Deposit])

Business Value:
This dashboard reduces manual errors, increases transparency, and enables instant cash position checks for each store, improving overall financial control.

Tech Stack: Google Sheets, Power BI Desktop, Power Query, DAX.
